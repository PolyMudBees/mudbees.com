Baja SAE Team


What is SAE Mini-Baja?

The SAE Mini-Baja is a prototype single-seater off-road vehicle capable of surviving the most grueling race conditions, built entirely by engineering students from Polytechnique Montréal. The team, composed of approximately twenty students, must design, manufacture, assemble and test the prototype in order to participate in competitions organized by the Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE). Therefore, the vehicle must comply to SAE rules and be powered only by a Briggs & Stratton 10 HP engine .

This extracurricular and entirely volunteer project allows to develop team work, management, design, mechanical manufacturing skills and allows us to apply the theoretical knowledge learned in the classroom in building a faster vehicle.

History of SAE Mini-Baja Competitions

The first Mini-Baja competition was held in 1976, but it was only in 1978 that the competition unfolds as we know it today: with an imposed engine by Briggs & Stratton , SAE safety regulations, different race courses and amphibious vehicles. That year, 10 universities North Americans competed.

The team archives allow us to go back to 1984 to the first participation of Polytechnique Montréal in the Mini-Baja competition. Since then, Polytechnique Montréal participates in competitions every year. And year after year the team creates a different, innovative and faster vehicle, allowing us to rank among the best teams of Mini-Baja.

Competitions

About

The goal of this technical society is to participate and perform well in different competitions. Three competitions a year are organized by the SAE and two competitions are organized by universities.

The competitions of the SAE allow us to stand out on several levels: the static, dynamic and endurance tests. Static tests consist of a mandatory technical inspection of the vehicle, conditional to the participation in the dynamic tests and the design and marketing presentations. Dynamic tests are often extreme in order to test the reliability and agility of the vehicle. There is, among other things, an acceleration test, a maneuverability test, a hill climb, a tractor pull, most of which occur in difficult environments such as rocks or mud. The endurance test consists of a four hour race on an offroad track that puts the vehicle in grueling race conditions.

The winter competition "L'Épreuve du Nord" is an event on snow and ice that often takes place between different teams Canada. This winter competition is quite different from the summer competitions, as the behavior of the vehicle is quite different.
In competition, all tasks are achieved by team members, from static presentations to piloting, to vehicle repairs or the organization of the different events. In addition, several team members participate in trackside assistance, which is a very entertaining task.

Sponsors

Visibility

The team is committed to giving as much visibility as possible to its various sponsors. To do this, the vehicle will participate in several events. The Baja will be seen by the general public, proudly displaying its sponsors.

In addition to the promotional events, the team will participate in three official competitions organized by the SAE. During these events, the team will give visibility to its sponsors, through promotional material such as jerseys and stickers on the vehicle.

In addition to off-campus presence, The Poly Mud Bees Baja team is always present at promotional events at Polytechnique MontrÉal, such as open house events, and is committed to getting new students interested in projects like Baja as well. It allows the application of the concepts learned at school in addition to developing autonomy. During these events the team proudly displays the list of its partners to the future engineers of the school.
